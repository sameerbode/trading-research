CRM Exit Strategy
Channel Rejection + Momentum Exit · V3.0 · Active
Current Rules — V3.0
Bias
1H EMA34 vs EMA50 — 730d seeded, shift(1). EMA34 > EMA50 = BULLISH (longs only). EMA34 < EMA50 = BEARISH (shorts only).
Entry
15m candle touches the 1H 34/50 EMA channel (wick or close) AND closes outside in the bias direction. BEARISH: close < min(EMA34,EMA50). BULLISH: close > max(EMA34,EMA50). Requires close to exceed channel edge by at least $0.01.
Exit
15m EMA8 crosses EMA21 in the opposite direction of the trade. No other exit condition.
Session
CST RTH only — 8:30 AM to 3:00 PM. Partial days (< 20 bars) excluded.
Options
ATM put/call. Strike: nearest $5 increment. Expiry: next Friday >= 2 weeks from entry. IV: HV20. Model: Black-Scholes. r=4.5%.
Key Insights
💡 Removing the opposite side close exit was the single biggest improvement — win rate jumped from 26% to 65% on TSLA
💡 8/21 exit beats 34/50 (holds too long, theta destroys options) and 5/12 (fires too fast, misses continuation)
💡 730d seeding for 1H EMAs is required to match TradingView values — 60d seeding causes divergence
💡 Thin channels (EMA34/50 width < $0.20) produce consistent noise trades — filter candidate
💡 Options amplify stock moves dramatically: 5%+ stock move = 85-94% option gain at 14-17 DTE
💡 Stock losers become outsized options losers: -3.5% stock = -64% options. Risk management critical.
💡 14-18 DTE at entry is the sweet spot with 8/21 exit timing
